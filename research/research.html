Localized functionalization and immobilization of carbon platforms for bio-sensing application


Localized functionalization and immobilization of carbon platforms for bio-sensing application
This project involves:
  • Optimization of plasma treatment and plasma polymerization for localized treatment of suspended carbon nano-wires
  • Thorough analysis of the functionalized platforms immediately after treatment and over an extended period of time for assuring that the functionalized platform does not suffer from aging effect
  • Application of the activated platform for protein immobilization and subsequent electrochemical detection of infectious diseases
  • Transferring the optimized functionalization strategy to other carbon structures including nano-constrictions and nano-gaps.

3-D printed artery-on-chip for studying atherosclerosis


3-D printed artery-on-chip for studying atherosclerosis
This project involves:
  • Design and fabrication of a lab-on-a-chip device that mimics different types of flow (e.g. laminar, helical, and chaotic) for creating sheer stress on the walls of an artificial artery
  • Design and fabrication of an artery that closely mimics the natural artery. The 3D printed artery will be fabricated by using MRI scans of artery and with the aim of a bioprinter
  • The 3D printed artery would be integrated within the lab-on-a-chip platform and the HUVEC cells would be cultured within the artificial artery
  • The media would run through the artificial artery simulating different flow profiles and the formation of atherosclerosis will be studies in a realistic manner

ELISA on contact lens: a continuous monitoring of diabetic retinopathy.


ELISA on contact lens: a continuous monitoring of diabetic retinopathy.
This project involves:
  • Design and fabrication of a set of contact lenses for non-invasive biosensing
  • The contact lens contains a hydrophilic and a hydrophobic biocompatible layer for continuous collection of tear while preserving samples in between the two layers
  • The middle layer is composed of a bioreceptor surface with immobilized enzyme-conjugated antibodies against the targeted disease (diabetic retinopathy)
  • The tear samples interact with the antibodies and the color will be indicated in the surrounding of the contact lens if the biomarker is present in the sample
  • The color formation and progression correspond to the concertation of the analyte of interest within the tear sample
